We Don't Know Where Our Leads Come From — And How to Fix It
5 min read  ·  Lead Tracking  ·  By PipelineX

If you're a trades or construction business owner running ads, you've probably said this at some point: "I'm not sure which one of these is actually bringing in jobs." You're spending money on Google, maybe running some Facebook ads, and your mate told you to try Instagram. Jobs are coming in — but you genuinely don't know which channel is sending them.

This is one of the most common problems we see when we onboard a new client. And it's fixable in a week.

Why This Happens

Most trades businesses set up ads through whoever built their website, or used a one-size-fits-all agency who never bothered to close the attribution loop. The ads run, the phone rings, money gets spent — but there's no system connecting the two.

Without tracking, you're essentially flying blind. You might be spending £1,000/month on a Google campaign that's generating nothing, while a £200 Meta test is booking 80% of your jobs.

The Three-Part Fix We Use for Every Client

1. UTM Parameters on Every Link
Every ad, every channel, every campaign gets a unique tracking tag. When someone clicks your ad and fills in a form, we know exactly which ad sent them. Set these up in Google Analytics and your CRM.

2. Unique Phone Numbers Per Channel
Use call tracking numbers (tools like CallRail or ResponseTap) so each marketing channel has its own number. When someone calls, you know whether they came from Google, Meta, or your website.

"The first thing we do for every new client is install proper tracking. Usually within the first two weeks, they discover one channel is responsible for 70%+ of their leads — and they've been wasting budget everywhere else."

— Louis Loftus, PipelineX

3. One Form Question That Changes Everything
Add "How did you hear about us?" to every enquiry form. Give specific options: Google Ad, Facebook Ad, Instagram, Referral, Google Search. This manual attribution catches everything your tracking might miss — especially phone-first customers who searched Google, called directly, and never clicked a tracked link.

What to Do With This Data

Once you have two to four weeks of data, patterns emerge fast. Kill the channels with no ROI. Double down on the ones that are booking jobs. For most of our clients, this single exercise reduces wasted ad spend by 30–40% within 30 days.

Getting Leads That Don't Convert? Your Follow-Up Is Too Slow
6 min read  ·  AI & Automation  ·  By PipelineX

You ran the ads. The leads came in. And then... nothing. They don't answer when you call back. They've "gone with someone else." Sound familiar?

This is the single biggest problem we see with trades businesses: the lead arrives, and by the time anyone responds, it's already dead.

The 5-Minute Rule

Research from Harvard Business Review found that responding to a lead within 5 minutes makes you 100x more likely to convert them than if you wait 30 minutes. Wait a day? You're essentially handing the job to your competitor.

For a heating engineer on site at 9am, that's a real problem. You can't drop tools every time a form comes in. So leads pile up, responses happen hours later, and the prospect has already booked someone else.

One of our clients — a boiler installation company in Leeds — was getting 20+ enquiries a week but converting fewer than 4. Their response time averaged 6 hours. We installed an AI follow-up system. Within 30 days, conversion rate doubled. Response time: under 4 minutes. 24/7.

What AI Follow-Up Actually Looks Like

It's not a chatbot that says "Hi! How can I help you today?" It's a smart SMS and email sequence that fires the moment a lead submits a form:

  • Immediate SMS — personalised to the enquiry type (boiler service, new install, emergency call-out)
  • Qualification questions — filters out tyre-kickers before your team spends time on a call
  • Calendar booking link — lets the qualified lead book directly into your diary
  • Follow-up sequence — if they don't respond, automated nudges go out at 2 hours, 24 hours, 72 hours

What This Costs to Set Up

Less than you think. Using tools like GoHighLevel, a basic AI follow-up system can be running within a week. The software costs around £100–£150/month. For most of our clients, one additional booked job per month covers the cost entirely.

The question isn't whether you can afford to automate follow-up. It's whether you can afford not to — while your competitors are responding in minutes.

Struggling to Structure Your Sales Calls? Your Funnel Is Missing This Step
7 min read  ·  Google & Meta Ads  ·  By PipelineX

Most trades businesses run ads that send people directly to a contact page. Someone clicks, sees a phone number, maybe fills in a form. Then your sales team calls them cold — no context, no qualification, no idea if this person is ready to buy or just shopping around.

The result? You spend 30 minutes on a call with someone who wanted a quote "just to compare prices" and had no intention of booking.

The fix is a qualification funnel — and it sits between your ad and your phone number.

What a Qualification Funnel Actually Is

A qualification funnel is a short series of questions that filters leads before they reach your diary. By the time someone books a call, you already know:

  • What job they need done
  • Their rough budget range
  • Their timeline (urgent vs. planning ahead)
  • Their location (are they even in your area?)
  • Whether they've had quotes before and what happened

This turns a cold inbound call into a warm, pre-qualified conversation. Your close rate goes up. Your time wasted goes down.

For a plumbing and heating client we work with, adding a qualification funnel before their booking page cut their "no-show" call rate from 40% to under 8%, and increased their average job value by 22% — because pre-qualified leads skew toward bigger jobs.

The Difference Between a £5 Lead and a £500 Lead

It's not the ad. Seriously — we've seen the same ad creative generate both. The difference is what happens after the click.

A £5 lead comes from an ad that sends people to a generic page, collects a name and number, and dumps them into a spreadsheet. A £500 lead comes from a structured funnel that qualifies intent, filters geography, confirms budget, and delivers a pre-warmed prospect ready to book.

How to Build One Over a Weekend

You don't need expensive software. A basic qualification funnel can be built using:

  • Typeform or GoHighLevel — for the multi-step form
  • Calendly or GHL Calendar — for booking
  • Zapier — to connect the form to your CRM and trigger automations

Map out 5–7 questions. Keep each one on its own screen. Use conditional logic to skip irrelevant questions. End with a calendar booking for people who pass your criteria, and a "we'll be in touch" message for everyone else.
